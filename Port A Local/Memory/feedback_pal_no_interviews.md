**PAL editorial does NOT conduct interviews with subjects of pieces.** Facts come from public records, court filings, on-record media quotes, public-meeting comments, FOIA documents, official agendas + minutes, and archival material.

**Why:** People can say anything they want. The facts can be displayed in any way. Letting subjects pre-spin a piece through curated quotes weakens credibility + fairness. PAL's editorial position is built on what's documented — not on what someone said off-the-record or in a quote PAL solicited. Locked by Winston 2026-04-28 during the P&Z capture Dispatch research.

**How to apply:**
- When researching a Dispatch or Heritage piece, **don't recommend "interview with X"** as a follow-up step. Don't include it in fact-base "next research path" lists.
- DO recommend: FOIA / Texas Public Information Act requests, county clerk / appraisal district / SoS entity filings, court-record searches, archival newspaper morgues, public-meeting minute reviews, IRS Form 990 pulls (for nonprofits), and database queries.
- Use existing on-record quotes from local journalism (South Jetty, Caller-Times, KIII-TV, KRIS-TV, Texas Tribune, etc.) freely — those are public-domain editorial material.
- Public-meeting commenters' on-record statements (council, P&Z, planning hearings) are fair game.
- This is editorial style, not blanket — Winston himself can talk to anyone he wants for context. The rule is about what's PUBLISHABLE: only sourced-from-records material lands in the piece.
