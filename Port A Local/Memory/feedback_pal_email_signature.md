**Strengthened 2026-05-03 PM after the Chris Jordan catering email opened with "Winston here at Port A Local" — that was a miss.** Going forward: every PAL-outbound email is entity-voice from start to finish. No "Winston here," no "I'm Winston with PAL," no first-name openers, no individual attribution anywhere in the message. The rule is symmetric: opener AND signature AND body — all entity.

The single sign-off is exactly:

> — The Port A Local

No trailing "team." No individual name. Em-dash, capital T, capital P, capital L.

**Override:** Winston can explicitly authorize personal voice for a specific message — "send this from me directly" or "this one comes from Winston." Default is always entity. Don't assume an exception even when the recipient knows Winston personally (Chris Jordan was a relationship intro and STILL gets entity voice unless Winston says otherwise).

**Why:** Winston wants PAL to read as both an entity and a single person simultaneously — ambiguous by design. A "team" sign-off makes it corporate; a personal name breaks the editorial/anti-Bureau posture where PAL speaks as *the* voice of the place rather than any individual operator. "The Port A Local" holds both at once — it's the publication, and it's also how a local would describe themselves in the third person. Naming Winston (or anyone) in the opener collapses that ambiguity and makes the operator visible — which defeats the design.

**How to apply:**
- All transactional email HTML templates (rent, beach, maintenance flows) — closing line entity-voice; no operator name in body either.
- Outbound vendor outreach (Chris-style emails) — open with the recipient's name + "Port A Local here" or similar entity phrasing, NOT "Winston here at PAL."
- Customer responses and replies to inquiries — same rule.
- Auto-responder templates for admin@ / hello@ / bookings@ — entity-voice throughout.
- Any cover copy, footer, or in-message reference.

**Bad opener examples (do NOT use):**
- "Winston here at Port A Local…"
- "I'm Winston with PAL…"
- "Hey Chris, Winston from Port A Local here…"

**Good opener examples:**
- "Chris, Port A Local here — quick proposal."
- "Port A Local reaching out about a catering opportunity."
- "Chris — we'd like to send catering leads your way." (no opener self-identification needed when context makes it clear)
