Every email sent or drafted from Port A Local — whether in code (transactional routes, email templates) or drafted in-thread for Winston to paste — signs off with exactly:

> — The Port A Local

No trailing "team." No individual name (not "Winston," not "the PAL team"). Em-dash, capital T, capital P, capital L.

**Why:** Winston wants the signature to read as both an entity and a single person simultaneously — ambiguous by design. A "team" sign-off makes it corporate; a personal name breaks the editorial/anti-Bureau posture where PAL speaks as *the* voice of the place rather than any individual operator. "The Port A Local" holds both at once — it's the publication, and it's also how a local would describe themselves in the third person.

**How to apply:**
- All transactional email HTML templates (rent, beach, maintenance flows) — closing line.
- Draft emails for Winston to send (vendor outreach, customer responses, replies to inquiries like Miguel's).
- Auto-responder templates for admin@ / hello@ / bookings@ workspace accounts.
- Any cover copy or footer where a sign-off is needed.

**Example (Miguel reply):**
> Miguel, great to hear from you — you're already in. Portable Detail Service is now listed under Services on theportalocal.com. No fee, no contract, no hoops. If you want to update anything (hours, service list, photos, direct booking), just reply to this email.
>
> — The Port A Local
