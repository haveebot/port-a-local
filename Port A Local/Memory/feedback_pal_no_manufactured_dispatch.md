**Rule:** Do not invent Dispatch story ideas by drawing correlations between what Winston is asking about and unrelated work I happen to have just shipped. If Winston asks for a Dispatch, the angle comes from his brief — the news hook, the topic, the trigger he names. Don't park "useful research" for future Dispatch pieces that were my idea, not his.

**Why:** Winston flagged this 2026-04-24 after I pitched a "Two Landlord Systems" Dispatch angle that tried to correlate Heritage #19 (red snapper IFQ) with his actual ask (a separate Dispatch on PA property taxes). He sent an email about property taxes; I riffed on snapper-as-Dispatch because the Heritage piece was freshly top-of-mind, spawned a research agent on it, and mentally filed the result for later. His response: *"it does not go into the parking lot. we do not need a made up dispatch story about snapper."* Manufacturing angles dilutes the editorial bar and wastes cycles. Heritage is Heritage. If a Heritage piece has a live current-events ember in it, Winston will say so — otherwise it stays preservation-only.

**How to apply:**
- On every Dispatch request: ground the angle strictly in Winston's brief + any public news trigger he cites. Do not import material from recently shipped Heritage pieces unless he asks for the bridge.
- Do not "park" Dispatch candidates I dreamed up. If a research thread becomes orphaned because I was on the wrong topic, let it die — do not save it as a "future piece."
- When the inbox has unread mail from Winston or Collie, READ IT FIRST before making inferences about what the current task is. I missed uids 157/158 ("PA Property Taxes") for hours because I never re-checked the inbox after my earlier session-start catch-up. Re-check when switching tasks.
- Distinguish Heritage from Dispatch. Heritage = preservation-first, historical, non-adversarial. Dispatch = current-events, pointed, requires a dated trigger + defensible sourcing. See `feedback_pal_dispatch_workflow.md`.
