Four customer-service-recovery sales patterns used during the PAL beach-rental canonical Cabana customer arc (2026-05-09, eight SMS over 48 minutes, "we want a refund" → "thank you"). Filed as HeyeDeploy framework pattern at `heyedeploy/patterns/customer-recovery-sales-patterns.md`.

**The four patterns:**

1. **"More than" reframe** to counter "half ass" / inadequate-delivery framing — don't deny, assert the positive harder with concrete differentiation
2. **Contradiction-pivot** when caught — find the variable that makes both prior statements true (PAL: "standard rig isn't suited for wet conditions; the one being set up for you now is")
3. **Push-through over refund-acknowledgment** when delivery is feasible — don't engage refund language, push forward with delivery story, let real value reframe
4. **Tone trajectory matching — never warmer than the customer** — match emotional state, don't lead with softness during hostile middle

**Two riders:** identification flip (ask how *they* should be identified) + contextual close (read what they volunteered — Mother's Day weekend close at PAL was triggered by "mom" in the ID cue + the May 9–10 booking dates).

**Why:** Every services-tier HeyeDeploy tenant will hit recovery moments. PAL canonical, Bron's next, future bookings tenants after that.

**How to apply:**
- When reviewing operator-drafted customer-facing outbound during a service-recovery scenario, check whether the draft uses one of the four patterns appropriately
- Decision rule: push through only when delivery is feasible; acknowledge the refund first when it isn't
- Pair with `feedback_substance_flag_before_grammar.md` — substance check before language polish on every reviewed reply

**When NOT to use:** delivery genuinely impossible / refund is the honest answer / frustration is about something other than the immediate delivery / customer demands phone and you can honor it.
