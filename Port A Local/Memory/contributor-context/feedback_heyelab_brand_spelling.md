**Rule:** Use **HeyeLab** (one word) for any visible brand surface. Use **Heye Lab** (two words) only in legal / official / placement contexts.

**Why:** Locked 2026-05-01 PM by Winston. The two forms serve different audiences. The brand mark is HeyeLab — that's how it appears in display type, wordmarks, page titles, hero copy, footer attribution chains ("Powered by HeyeLab · Built on `<Vertical>Deploy`"), and all marketing surfaces. The two-word "Heye Lab" form is reserved for formal contexts where the official corporate-name spelling is the right thing to surface (legal documents, IRS filings, board minutes, contracts).

The domain `heyelab.com` (no separator) reflects the one-word form. Nick Merrill's existing portfolio site at heyelab.com uses the one-word form ("Heyelab"). The brand-tokens file at `heyedeploy/brand/tokens.md` codifies this rule.

**Origin (brand history, locked 2026-05-04):** "Heye" is **Nick Merrill's paternal family name** — his father's family was Heye. The unconventional spelling is therefore not a stylistic invention or SEO play; it's a heritage name. This matters for:

- **Founder-narrative depth** in vendor outreach + press: the company is named after Nick's father's family, not concocted for a domain
- **Authority of the spelling lock**: the spelling is genealogically determined, not subject to "let's try a cleaner alternative" drift
- **Customer-facing pronunciation guidance**: when customers ask how to say "Heye," the answer can be "rhymes with [appropriate guidance based on Nick's family pronunciation]" — recall the family-name origin in case future sessions need to clarify
- **Cross-project cohesion**: HeyeLab → HeyeDeploy → component <X>Deploy chain all carry the family name forward; rebranding away from "Heye" would sever this

**How to apply:**

| Context | Form |
|---|---|
| Wordmark / logomark | **HeyeLab** |
| Display type, hero copy | **HeyeLab** |
| Page titles, og:site_name | **HeyeLab** |
| Footer chain "Powered by ___" | **Powered by HeyeLab** |
| Marketing copy, body | **HeyeLab** (default) |
| Legal docs, IRS filings | **Heye Lab** |
| Contracts, formal correspondence | **Heye Lab** |
| Internal docs, decision logs | Either is fine; default to HeyeLab |

When unsure, default to **HeyeLab** (one word).

**Don't:** Use "Heyelab" (lowercase L) — that's a different rendering Nick uses for his personal portfolio. The brand mark is camel-case **HeyeLab** with capital H and capital L.
