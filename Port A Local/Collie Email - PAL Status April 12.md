Port A Local started as a business directory. It is now a full-scale tourism platform. Live at port-a-local.vercel.app, domain purchased at theportalocal.com (waiting on Google Workspace to clear the DNS).

---

**WHAT'S BUILT**

**Directory**
- 140+ locally vetted businesses across 6 categories (Eat, Drink, Stay, Do, Fish, Shop)
- Every business has a detail page with hours, address, phone, tags, and a Google Maps link
- 127 businesses geocoded with real coordinates on an interactive map

**Gully — "Just Gully It"**
- Our own branded search engine. Searches businesses, heritage stories, and menu items from one search bar
- Cmd+K command palette on every page (like Spotlight on Mac)
- ~405 menu items indexed — someone searches "fried shrimp" and gets restaurants that serve it
- 732 enriched tags (happy hour, pet friendly, live music, sunset views, etc.)
- Recent searches saved locally
- Dedicated homepage section + nav search pill

**Port A Heritage — 17 Published Stories**
- The first organized digital history of Port Aransas. 17 original long-form editorial pieces totaling ~25,000 words
- Covers 5,000 years — from the Karankawa to Cinnamon Shore
- Includes the Farley Boat Works stories (both the family history piece and a deep craft piece on the no-blueprint method)
- Cross-linked between stories, integrated into Gully search
- Dynamic social sharing images for every story (when shared on Facebook/Twitter, shows a branded card)

**Historical Photo Archives — 31 Photos**
- The largest organized digital collection of Port Aransas historical photographs online
- Sourced from Library of Congress, National Archives, NOAA, NASA, UTSA, University of Houston, Wikimedia Commons, Texas Historical Commission
- Spans 1853 to 2017 — Civil War maps, the 1919 hurricane, Russell Lee FSA photos from 1939, Hurricane Celia, Hurricane Harvey aerials from the Texas National Guard
- Every photo linked to source institution with proper attribution
- Cross-linked to related heritage stories

**Curated Guides — 10 Guides**
- Auto-generated from our tag data: Best Happy Hours, Pet-Friendly, Date Night, Family-Friendly, Sunset Views, Live Music, Seafood, Outdoor Adventures, Locally Owned, Late Night
- Each guide page shows matching businesses with count

**Trip Planner — My Trip**
- Save/bookmark any business or heritage story by tapping a heart icon
- No account needed — saves in the browser
- My Trip page shows saved places + heritage reading list

**Island Pulse — Live Conditions**
- 10 webcam links (beach cams, ferry cam, harbor cams)
- MarineTraffic embedded map showing real-time ship traffic in the channel
- Native NOAA weather dashboard — water temp, air temp, wind speed/direction, wind gust
- Today's tide predictions (high/low times and heights)

**Interactive Map**
- Every business plotted on a real map with category-colored pins
- 127 geocoded addresses (3 rounds of refinement for accuracy)
- Click a pin for name, tagline, and link to listing

**Island Essentials**
- 10-section arrival guide: ferry info, golf cart rules, beach rules, parking, weather safety, wildlife, fishing basics, dining tips, emergency contacts, connectivity
- Sticky quick-jump navigation
- The page every visitor saves to their phone before they get on the ferry

**Events & Happenings**
- 15 major annual events organized by month (SandFest, Deep Sea Roundup, Beachtober, Wooden Boat Festival, Lighted Boat Parade, etc.)
- 4 recurring events (Second Saturday at Farley, Art Center First Friday, UTMSI tours, live music nightly)

**Fishing Report**
- Real-time NOAA conditions at the top
- Species by season (Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter)
- 6 types of fishing with descriptions (Bay, Surf, Offshore, Pier, Jetty, Kayak)

**Where to Stay**

**Community Photos**
