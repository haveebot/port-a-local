## A2P 10DLC campaign resubmitted (failure → fix → new submission)

**Failure surfaced:** Winston flagged that the campaign came back FAILED. Queried Twilio API directly (`/v1/Services/{sid}/Compliance/Usa2p`) and pulled the rejection: **error 30923 on MESSAGE_FLOW** — "consent cannot be a required condition for service or transaction completion." Our three forms (maintenance, rent, beach) bundled SMS consent into the form-submit action: *"By submitting, you agree to… and consent to receive SMS"* — TCR reads that as required consent, which violates CTIA rules.

**Fix shipped (commit 132b312):**
- **Forms:** Added an unchecked-by-default SMS opt-in checkbox above each submit button: *"Text me confirmations and updates about this request (optional). Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out."* Removed SMS language from the submit-button legal line — only Terms + Privacy agreement remains there.
- **Backend:** New `src/lib/twilioSms.ts` with `sendSms` (internal/vendor, unconditional) and `sendConsumerSms` (gated on `smsConsent === true` — accepts boolean from direct POST or `"true"`/`"false"` string from Stripe metadata round-trip).
- **API wiring:** Replaced duplicate SMS functions across 4 routes with the shared helper. Gated customer SMS on `smsConsent` in maintenance direct-POST, maintenance confirm (Stripe path), rent confirm, and beach confirm. `smsConsent` threaded through all 3 Stripe checkout routes in metadata.
- **New capability:** Added customer SMS to rent + beach confirmations (was email-only before). So opted-in customers now get SMS on all three revenue flows.
- **Customer SMS copy:** All consumer messages now end with "Reply STOP to opt out." for belt-and-suspenders compliance.
- **Vendor/ops SMS is unaffected:** John Brown dispatch, admin alerts, cart vendor blasts are internal B2B comms, different lane.

**Twilio-side (done via REST API, no code):**
- Account `friendly_name`: "My first Twilio account" → **"Port A Local"** (was just the Twilio default; not an A2P blocker, but unprofessional on invoices).
- Messaging service `friendly_name`: "Low Volume Mixed A2P Messaging Service" → **"Port A Local — Transactional SMS"**.
- Deleted the FAILED Usa2p compliance record (`QE2c6890da…`).
- Submitted a new Usa2p record with:
  - Rewritten **MessageFlow** that explicitly describes the separate-opt-in checkbox UX.
  - 5 fresh consumer-facing **sample messages** — all with "Reply STOP to opt out." — covering maintenance confirmation, priority dispatch, cart reservation, beach booking, and pre-arrival pickup reminder.
  - Expanded **Description** covering the three use cases.
