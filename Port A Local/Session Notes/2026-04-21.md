### Legal framework conversation (the 4/15 marketplace pivot made explicit)

Collie flagged the legal gap ("Legal Eagle Stuff — may need to add Assumption of risk / release of liability / indemnification…"). Talked through what the marketplace model actually requires to hold up:

- **Platform T&C** that explicitly position PAL as a marketplace (not the service provider); customer's service contract is with the vendor; PAL disclaims liability for vendor actions; customer assumes risk of the rental itself.
- **Vendor agreements** — one-pager per vendor: independent contractor, carries insurance, indemnifies PAL, honors the discount + service commitments.
- **Actual vetting** — if marketing says "vetted local companies," insurance verification has to be real (misrepresentation risk otherwise).

**Decision:** engage a Texas business attorney for a one-time review of T&C + vendor agreements + indemnification language. Rationale: Texas has specific indemnification doctrines (fair notice, express negligence test) that generic templates often fail. $500–1,500 one-time is cheap vs. one slip-and-sue. Without this paperwork, a plaintiff's lawyer can argue PAL operated as the provider, not a marketplace, and a judge may agree. With it, the vendor-liability thesis holds cleanly (same model as Airbnb, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor).
