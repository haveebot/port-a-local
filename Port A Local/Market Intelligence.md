## The Market — Port Aransas, TX

### Who's Coming
- Heavy tourist traffic, especially spring and summer
- Visitors are largely Texas-based (Houston, San Antonio, Austin, DFW)
- Mix of families, fishing groups, couples, college crowds
- Many are repeat visitors — Port A has a loyal following
- Visitors heavily rely on their phones for on-island decisions

### How They Find Things
- Historically: word of mouth. No single trusted source exists.
- Google searches for generic terms ("restaurants in Port Aransas", "golf cart rental Port Aransas")
- Facebook groups (Port A visitors groups are active)
- TripAdvisor, Yelp — present but not dominant
- **Opportunity:** Port A Local can own local search if SEO is built correctly

### What They Want
- Quick, trustworthy answers — not a wall of ads
- "Where should we eat tonight?" — discovery, not search
- Convenience — book it, don't think about it
- Local authenticity — they're tired of chains and tourist traps

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors
- **None identified yet** — no single trusted local directory for Port A exists

- Port Aransas Chamber of Commerce has a directory but it's basic and not user-friendly

### Adjacent Competitors
- **Golf cart rental:** Multiple vendors exist (JOY, The Cart Store, Bron's Beach Carts, etc.)
  - All book direct — no platform aggregating them
  - Opportunity: own the booking layer above them
- **Beach rentals:** Informal market — no dominant player identified yet
- **Maintenance:** No local lead-gen platform for contractors in Port A

### Advantage
- Local curation — "vetted by people who live here" is a real differentiator
- Platform model — we're above the vendors, not competing with them
- Speed — we can move faster than any competitor that isn't just two people
