# Charles Bujan — Mayor Through Harvey

> Research compiled April 2026 for Port A Local Heritage piece (#23). Subject: Charles Robert Bujan (1944–2022), the three-term mayor of Port Aransas who led the island through Hurricane Harvey and its long, slow recovery. Pitched to designer/editor Collie for approval before drafting.

---

## Subject + scope

The piece covers Bujan's life from his ferry-ride birth in 1944 through his tenure on city council (2007–2014, four terms) and as mayor (2016–2022, three terms), with the meatiest section anchored in his Hurricane Harvey leadership (Aug. 24, 2017 evacuation order through the four-and-a-half years of recovery he oversaw before his death from pancreatic cancer on Feb. 9, 2022).

The recommended framing is **Heritage** rather than Dispatch. Bujan died in 2022; the recovery he started is now folded into the city's institutional memory; his name is on the new $12 million Public Safety Building (ribbon-cut April 2026). There is no current news hook. Heritage is the right home — this is preserved local history.

The piece also has an evergreen pull: of the five great destructive storms in Port Aransas history (1916, 1919, 1961 Carla, 1970 Celia, 2017 Harvey), Bujan is the only mayor whose individual stewardship is well-documented in the public record. He is the human face of how Port A survives.

## Timeline

- **Aug. 9, 1944** — Charles Robert Bujan born during a ferry crossing between Port Aransas and Corpus Christi. Mother Viola goes into labor on the ferry to the mainland hospital. ([Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan), [South Jetty profile](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **1940s–50s** — Grows up in Port Aransas. Goes to Port A schools through 8th grade, then Aransas Pass High School (where he is quarterback on the football team and drives a maroon '57 Chevy). ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **Late 1960s–early 1970s** — Works as parts expeditor at Cessna and Lear aircraft manufacturing plants in Wichita. Joins U.S. Air Force; serves four-plus years in communications, stationed in Idaho, Turkey, West Germany, and Sicily. Honorably discharged as sergeant. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/), [Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan))
- **Post-Air Force** — Returns to Port Aransas, attends Corpus Christi State University, earns bachelor's degree in economics. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **~1975–~2000 (approx. 25 years)** — Moves to Atlantic City, New Jersey. Works for Prudential Insurance Co. in personnel/HR; rises to associate manager. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **2006** — Returns home and runs for Port Aransas City Council. **Loses** to Bubba Jensen. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **2007** — Elected to City Council. Re-elected 2009, 2011, 2014 (four total two-year terms). ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **2007–~2024** — Volunteer Baptist chaplain's assistant at the William G. McConnell Unit prison in Beeville for ~17 years. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))
- **May 2016** — Elected mayor of Port Aransas. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/), [KRIS-TV](https://www.kristv.com/news/local-news/port-aransas-mayor-bujan-dies-at-77))
- **Aug. 23, 2017** — Watches a tropical disturbance form in the Bay of Campeche. Tells himself: *"there was no place it could come except right here in Port Aransas."* ([KUT/Texas Standard](https://www.kut.org/texas/2018-08-20/harvey-damaged-100-percent-of-port-aransas-businesses-but-the-island-town-is-working-its-way-back))
- **Aug. 24, 2017, ~10:30 a.m.** — At a meeting of city officials, Bujan states his intention to order an evacuation. The mandatory evacuation of Port Aransas's ~4,000 residents is set for **9 p.m. that night**. ([South Jetty: "Surreality of Harvey"](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/the-surreality-of-harvey/), [CCBizNews](https://www.ccbiznews.com/port_aransas_evacuating_for_hurricane_harvey))

## The man — bio facts

**Full legal name:** Charles Robert Bujan.

**Date of birth:** August 9, 1944. Born during a ferry crossing — his mother Viola went into labor on the ferry from Port Aransas to Corpus Christi (where the hospital was). He told the story his whole life that he was born *"between Port Aransas and Corpus Christi."* ([Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan))

**Date of death:** February 9, 2022, at his home in Port Aransas. Age 77. Cause: cancer (pancreatic, Stage 4 at diagnosis October 2021). ([Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan), [KRIS-TV](https://www.kristv.com/news/local-news/port-aransas-mayor-bujan-dies-at-77))

**Family roots in Port Aransas:** Multi-generational. The South Jetty profile traces him to **great-great-grandfather Franz Josef Frandolig**, who emigrated from **Hungary in 1815** and settled in what became Rockport, then moved to Tarpon (today's Port Aransas) in the late 1800s. **Grandfather Matteo Bujan was from Croatia.** Family has been on Mustang Island for **130+ years**. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/), [KUT](https://www.kut.org/texas/2018-08-20/harvey-damaged-100-percent-of-port-aransas-businesses-but-the-island-town-is-working-its-way-back))

**Parents:** Charles Clifford "Charlie" Bujan (1906–1970) — school janitor, pilot boat captain — and Viola Mildred Bujan. Bujan founded Beacon RV Park in 1945 (Charles eventually took it over). One older sister, Charlotte Roser (predeceased him). ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/), [Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan))

**Wife:** Sherri Bujan, married ~25 years at his death. Obituary calls her his *"soulmate and the love of his life."* ([Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan))

**Children/stepchildren:**
- Rob Bujan (with partner Jeffrey Perry)
- Jon Bujan
- Stepdaughter Jennifer Kinberger (with husband Garth Kinberger)
- Stepson Erik Thompson (with wife Tiffany Thompson)
([Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan))

**Grandchildren:** Ava, Jack, Clyde Sanders, Sean Thomas. ([Charlie Marshall obituary](https://www.charliemarshallfuneralhomes.com/obituaries/Charles-Bujan))

**Education:**
- Port Aransas schools through 8th grade
- Aransas Pass High School (quarterback)
- Bachelor's in economics, Corpus Christi State University (now Texas A&M–Corpus Christi)
([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))

**Military service:** U.S. Air Force, four-plus years, communications. Stationed in Idaho, Turkey, West Germany, and Sicily. Honorably discharged as sergeant. ([South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/barefoot-boy-to-three-term-mayor/))

**Career before politics:**
- Parts expeditor, Cessna and Lear plants, Wichita

## Harvey + the storm response

This is the meatiest section — the historical core.

### The week before

Bujan watched the disturbance form in the Bay of Campeche on **Aug. 23, 2017**. He told KUT later that he had a premonition about its track:

> *"As I watched it develop over time, I realized that there was no place it could come except right here in Port Aransas."*
> — Charles Bujan, [KUT/Texas Standard, Aug. 20, 2018](https://www.kut.org/texas/2018-08-20/harvey-damaged-100-percent-of-port-aransas-businesses-but-the-island-town-is-working-its-way-back)

### The evacuation order

Thursday morning, **Aug. 24, 2017**, around 10:30 a.m., Bujan met with city officials and stated his intention to order a mandatory evacuation. The order called for residents to leave by **9 p.m. that night** — about 12 hours of warning. Population at the time: ~4,000. ([South Jetty: Surreality of Harvey](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/the-surreality-of-harvey/), [CCBizNews](https://www.ccbiznews.com/port_aransas_evacuating_for_hurricane_harvey))

He later called it the most difficult call of his life:

> *"And I'm glad I did, otherwise we would've had a lot of body bags after the hurricane. Because we only had 12 hours to get everybody off here before this thing was a major storm. So that to me was the most difficult call of my life. I'll never forget it."*
> — Charles Bujan, [KUT/Texas Standard, Aug. 20, 2018](https://www.kut.org/texas/2018-08-20/harvey-damaged-100-percent-of-port-aransas-businesses-but-the-island-town-is-working-its-way-back)

**Bujan was one of the only area elected officials who ordered a mandatory evacuation ahead of Harvey's landfall** — Rockport's mayor did, but most Coastal Bend cities issued voluntary evacuations only. ([Yahoo/news syndication](https://news.yahoo.com/port-aransas-mayor-charles-bujan-183151271.html))

### Where Bujan was during the storm

Bujan stayed in **Corpus Christi** during the storm. He did not ride out the storm in Port Aransas itself.

The four city/county officials who **did** stay through landfall sheltered in the brick **UT Marine Science Institute** building:
- City Manager Dave Parsons
- Building Official Abel Carrillo
- Emergency Operations Coordinator Rick Adams
- EMS Director Tim McIntosh

They emerged the morning of **Aug. 26** to begin damage assessment. Adams later described his emotional shift during the storm as going *"from confident … to fearful."* Parsons texted the South Jetty editor at 2:53 a.m. Sunday: *"Town very smashed up. Not sure if this text will make it out??? Little to zero cell coverage."* ([South Jetty: Harvey: Hell & High Water](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/harvey-hell-high-water/), [KUT](https://www.kut.org/texas/2018-08-20/harvey-damaged-100-percent-of-port-aransas-businesses-but-the-island-town-is-working-its-way-back))

Bujan was unable to reach Port Aransas for hours after the storm because **debris covered State Highway 361**, the only road on/off Mustang Island. ([South Jetty: Harvey: Hell & High Water](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/harvey-hell-high-water/))

### Landfall and damage

Harvey made landfall near **10:00 p.m. CDT on Aug. 25, 2017** as a Category 4 hurricane. Eye first touched down on San Jose Island, then again at Rockport/Fulton.

Per NWS Corpus Christi:
- Sustained winds: 130 mph
- Peak gust: 145 mph at Aransas County Airport (8 m); 152 mph corrected to 10 m
- Storm surge in Port Aransas: 5.53 ft measured (regional max 12+ ft at Aransas Wildlife Refuge)
- ~6,600 Port Aransas homes impacted; 4,000+ major damage; 1,000+ destroyed; 450+ businesses major damage
([NWS Corpus Christi](https://www.weather.gov/crp/hurricane_harvey))

City-wide damage figures Bujan repeated publicly:
- 100% of Port Aransas businesses damaged
- 85% of homes damaged
- City infrastructure damage: $50–70 million (police station, library, municipal buildings)
- Total estimated damage: ~$1 billion
- Property tax base lost: ~$300 million
([KUT/Texas Standard](https://www.kut.org/texas/2018-08-20/harvey-damaged-100-percent-of-port-aransas-businesses-but-the-island-town-is-working-its-way-back))

### The first 72 hours

**Aug. 26, Saturday morning** — Officials reach town. Bujan tells KIII-TV in early statements simply: *"It's a mess."* Search-and-rescue crews operate in the area. ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/we-are-encouraging-residents-not-to-come-back-says-port-aransas-mayor/), [South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/harvey-hell-high-water/))

**Aug. 27, Sunday** — Bujan tells residents in a televised KIII-TV interview: *"We are encouraging residents not to come back."* He describes the trailer park as a *"100 percent loss."* ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/we-are-encouraging-residents-not-to-come-back-says-port-aransas-mayor/))

**Aug. 28, Monday** — Bujan and council host a press conference announcing checkpoint elimination and phased re-entry. Residents allowed back into town with curfew restrictions (initially noon–6 p.m.; later 6:30 a.m.–8 p.m.). ([South Jetty: Harvey: Hell & High Water](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/harvey-hell-high-water/))

On the residents who defied his evacuation order and stayed:
> *"I would say that was a major miracle… They lucked out, to be honest with you."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty: Harvey: Hell & High Water](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/harvey-hell-high-water/)

**Zero deaths in Port Aransas.** No fatalities were reported within the city limits — widely credited to Bujan's mandatory evacuation order. ([Yahoo](https://news.yahoo.com/port-aransas-mayor-charles-bujan-183151271.html))

## Recovery period

Recovery is where Bujan's stewardship deepened — and where his frustration with FEMA became a defining theme.

### One month later (Sept. 2017)

By late September, Bujan reported to TPR that water, sewer, and electricity had been restored. Debris cleanup was the priority and would take *"another month or two."* He was already preaching patience for the long haul:

> *"Years. … A lot of help from the government; state and federal."*
> — Charles Bujan on the recovery timeline, [TPR, Sept. 25, 2017](https://www.tpr.org/news/2017-09-25/rebuilding-after-harvey-the-road-to-recovery-for-port-aransas)

> *"We're a sea people, and we understand what the ocean can do. We choose to live here."*
> — Charles Bujan, [TPR, Sept. 25, 2017](https://www.tpr.org/news/2017-09-25/rebuilding-after-harvey-the-road-to-recovery-for-port-aransas)

### FEMA fight (late 2017–2020)

Bujan became one of the loudest voices among coastal mayors criticizing FEMA's pace and the Texas General Land Office's housing program.

In December 2017, testifying to the Texas House Appropriations Subcommittee on Disaster Impact and Recovery:

> *"They may have very well set you up to fail."*
> — Charles Bujan to state lawmakers, December 2017, [Texas Tribune, Feb. 27, 2018](https://www.texastribune.org/2018/02/27/texans-left-limbo-gov-abbott-fema-use-harvey-reinvent-disaster-respons/)

On FEMA's broader performance:
> *"They rank high on promises and way low on promises kept."*
> — Charles Bujan, [Texas Tribune](https://www.texastribune.org/2018/02/27/texans-left-limbo-gov-abbott-fema-use-harvey-reinvent-disaster-respons/)

On the FEMA-to-GLO housing handoff (Jan. 5, 2018, Port Aransas City Hall):
> *"They were handed a bad apple to begin with by FEMA. FEMA knew when they gave that project to the GLO that it was a very impossible thing. FEMA does not want to be in the housing business."*
> — Charles Bujan, [Texas Standard, Jan. 5, 2018](https://www.texasstandard.org/stories/were-resilient-people-port-aransas-mayor-says-city-is-recovering-despite-receiving-little-help/)

> *"They have been a tremendous failure."*
> — Charles Bujan on FEMA's mobile-home/trailer program, [Texas Standard, Jan. 5, 2018](https://www.texasstandard.org/stories/were-resilient-people-port-aransas-mayor-says-city-is-recovering-despite-receiving-little-help/)

On the funding inequity between Houston and the coast:
> *"Just recently our federal government released $54 million in grants. $47 million went to Houston. What does that leave for the rest of the coast?"*
> — Charles Bujan, [Texas Standard, Jan. 5, 2018](https://www.texasstandard.org/stories/were-resilient-people-port-aransas-mayor-says-city-is-recovering-despite-receiving-little-help/)

### One year later (Aug. 2018)

> *"One year later, I think we've made remarkable progress."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty: Leaving Harvey behind](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/leaving-harvey-behind/)

> *"On weekends, it's packed. On holidays, it's packed. That's a measurement that says to me: Hey, you're on the road."*
> — Charles Bujan on tourism's return, [South Jetty: Leaving Harvey behind](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/leaving-harvey-behind/)

The emotional toll he kept calling out:
> *"I'm concerned about the emotional impact this has had on our citizens. They have post-traumatic stress syndrome. … They're still afraid. … It's a life-changing event."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty: Leaving Harvey behind](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/leaving-harvey-behind/)

### Three years later (Aug. 2020)

> *"As far as city infrastructure is concerned, we're still way behind the eight ball on that, primarily because we have to deal with FEMA."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty: Three years later](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/three-years-later/)

> *"FEMA is slow. That's the problem. … It's really frustrating. It's slow as molasses running uphill."*

### Fiscal stewardship

Bujan was famous for refusing to raise taxes after Harvey, despite a $300M property-tax-base loss. He laid off 10% of city staff to save ~$700K. He also opposed any new hires and pursued grants instead of debt:

> *"My number one objective has been and will continue to be moving our city toward full recovery."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/airing-the-issues/)

> *"We have an AA+ plus rating and, as of February this year, we had $26 million in reserves."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/airing-the-issues/)

> *"No hiring at all while I'm in office. Not under this situation."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/airing-the-issues/)

> *"We need to make do with what we have."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/airing-the-issues/)

By 2020 he was personally tracking 55 grant and funding projects worth over $50 million. He had also reportedly lost 50 pounds in the years after Harvey from the workload — his self-reported figure. ([South Jetty: Airing the issues](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/airing-the-issues/))

## Other notable acts as mayor

### The Port of Corpus Christi / Harbor Island fight

Bujan led Port Aransas in a multi-year jurisdictional fight with the Port of Corpus Christi over a proposed crude-oil export terminal on Harbor Island that would have served Very Large Crude Carriers (VLCCs). The dispute spilled into the courts. Bujan's framing:

> The Port was trying *"to pressure the city to give up its right of governance of land within its city limits."*
> — Charles Bujan, [Marine Log](https://www.marinelog.com/news/port-of-corpus-christi-and-city-of-port-aransas-settle-harbor-island-spat/) / [Port of Corpus Christi statement](https://portofcc.com/port-of-corpus-christi-city-of-port-aransas-reach-settlement-on-harbor-island/)

The Council voted **7-0** to approve a settlement that included:
- Interlocal agreement framework
- Port Aransas oversight on city ordinance/permitting on Harbor Island
- Funding for fire protection and emergency services on Harbor Island ($1M for Port Aransas FD per CCBizNews)
- End of all litigation
- "A seat at the table" for Port Aransas in any future Harbor Island development
([CCBizNews on FD funding](https://www.ccbiznews.com/news/harbor-island-settlement-nets-1M-for-port-aransas-FD))

This was Bujan's "fighting for the little man" frame applied to a Goliath — a small island city standing up to one of the largest U.S. ports.

### The 2021 wish

> *"I'd like to have just a normal year. No crises. Seems like I've had crises ever since 2017."*
> — Charles Bujan, [South Jetty: The path forward](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/the-path-forward/)

(Said in early 2021. He had Harvey, then COVID. He would not get the normal year — the cancer diagnosis came in October 2021.)

### Succession

Bujan's third term was scheduled to end in May 2022. He was term-limited and could not run again. After his death on Feb. 9, 2022, Mayor Pro Tem **Joan Holt** served as interim mayor. **Wendy Moore** won the May 7, 2022 special election with 901 votes (over Martin J. Phalen and Gregory Chittum). ([CCBizNews](https://www.ccbiznews.com/news/may-7-election-results), [KRIS-TV](https://www.kristv.com/news/local-news/port-aransas-mayor-bujan-dies-at-77))
