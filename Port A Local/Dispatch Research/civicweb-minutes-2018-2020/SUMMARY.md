# Civicweb minutes pull — 2018-2020 — SUMMARY

_Pulled 2026-05-07 in response to the Texas Public Information Act reply from City Secretary on the 2019 Hotel + Conference Center procurement. Editorial gating question: was the grading rubric presented to council before the 7-0 vote on Jan 17, 2019?_

---

## Files in this directory

20 council agenda packets (Jan 2019 → Dec 2020), each saved as both PDF and extracted .txt for full-text search. Plus civicweb meeting-list HTML for cross-referencing document IDs.

---

## Q1 — Was the rubric attached to the Jan 17, 2019 packet?

**No.** Confidence: **High** for the publicly available packet.

**Smoking gun:** `2019-01-17-council-agenda-packet.pdf` page 71 of 413, Item 7-H commentary line reads literally:

> **"INFORMATION SENT SEPARATE BY CITY MANAGER FOR REVIEW"**

The substantive evaluation material was deliberately routed outside the public packet. PDF metadata: created Jan 11, 2019 — six days before the vote. Full-text search of the packet returns **zero hits** for `rubric`, `175`, `182`, `71.5`, or `74%`.

**Resolution template was a procedural shell, not a deliberative document.** It listed the wrong opponent ("McCombs Properties" instead of Palmilla) and left the awardee field blank.

**Corroborating timing finding:** Dec 13, 2018 minutes (embedded in the same Jan 17 packet) record then-City-Manager Hentz telling council the RFP committee was *"in final deliberation on the evaluation and grading of each proposal to be completed in the upcoming week."* The rubric was not even finished a month before the vote.

## Q2/Q3 — Was the rubric referenced in the meeting record? Vote language?

The actual Jan 17, 2019 minutes themselves are NOT publicly retrievable (see Blocker below). The Feb 21, 2019 packet's consent agenda lists them for ratification but the body only contains Jul/Aug/Sep/Nov 2018 minutes. Civicweb's City Council Regular list begins Dec 18, 2019.

What we have from press coverage: 7-0 vote awarding to Cinnamon Shore (with ZJZ as hospitality partner). The motion language and any rubric reference within the meeting itself is not in any publicly retrievable document and would require a TPIA follow-up to the City Secretary.

## Q4 — Was the project alive at Dec 31, 2020?

**Very much alive — but materially altered.**

### Jul 16, 2020 — Council voted 7-0 to relocate the conference center

From Cinnamon Shore II → **Newport Marina PUD (the Brookdale parcel)**. Verified via embedded minutes in `2020-10-15-council-agenda.pdf` lines 951-967.

Same Jul 16 meeting also approved a dune permit for **"PA Waterfront, LP, The Brookdale Group, LLC"** — confirming the Brookdale partnership.

### Nov 19, 2020 — Resolution 2020-R59 (Second Amendment)

Extended the Development Agreement with a new SPV: **"The Inn, Spa & Conference Center, LP."** Through Dec 31, 2022.

### Lamkin's developer timeline (in `2020-12-17-council-agenda.pdf` lines 700-755):

| Milestone | Target |
|---|---|
| Close on land | Feb 1, 2021 |
| PUD amendment | Aug 1, 2021 |
| Infrastructure design | Dec 31, 2021 |
| Groundbreaking | Dec 31, 2022 |

**Editorial implication:** The original Jan 17, 2019 award terms did NOT result in a built conference center at Cinnamon Shore. The project migrated to the Brookdale parcel under a new SPV with a new agreement. That migration is itself reportable — the public was sold one project location and got another, after closed-door deliberation.

## Sidebar findings worth noting

### Two of the seven voting council members were ALSO on the RFP committee

**Crawford and Moore** were on the RFP grading committee. Meaning: when the Jan 17, 2019 council voted 7-0, two of those seven votes came from members who had personally helped develop and apply the rubric. **The other five had not seen the rubric in any publicly distributed materials.**

This is editorially significant. The "seats and structure" thesis the P&Z Capture piece runs on now has a clean structural finding inside the procurement itself: the grading committee and the deciding council overlapped by 28.5%, and the grading product was kept off the public packet.

### Spelling inconsistencies in city documents

City documents inconsistently use:
- **Cinnamon Shore** vs. **Cinnamon Shores**
- **Lamkin** vs. **Lampkin**

Possibly worth a Dispatch sidebar — the carelessness of the official record on a $1.3B-impact procurement is itself a story shape.

## Blocker

The actual **Jan 17, 2019 minutes** are NOT in any publicly retrievable packet. Civicweb's City Council Regular list starts at Dec 18, 2019 — the city migrated to civicweb after Jan 17, 2019 and didn't backport the older record.

The civicweb document tree partially blocks unauthenticated access (many `/document/<id>/` URLs redirect to `/user/signin`).
