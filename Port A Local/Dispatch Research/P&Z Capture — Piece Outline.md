## Lede candidates (3 versions)

### Lede A — The Crawford / CCMS-700 / 2023-rescission lede (RECOMMENDED)

> On September 21, 2023, the Port Aransas City Council rescinded a two-year-old ordinance that had required short-term-rental owners to live on site before renting an accessory dwelling. Sitting in Place #5 was Charles Crawford Jr. — president of CCMS LLC, a property-management firm that operates more than 700 vacation rentals in Port Aransas. There is no indexed Form CIQ disclosing the overlap. There is no indexed recusal. There is the vote.

*Why this is the strongest lede:* it is the cleanest single-event opening — a member, a matter, a vote — and the ratios (CCMS = 700+ units; year-round population = 2,904) force the reader to carry the structural question themselves. Sources: [South Jetty: City to consider residency for STRs](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/city-to-consider-residency-for-strs/); [Port Aransas Chamber listing for CCMS LLC](https://www.portaransas.org/listing/ccms-llc/100/); [BBB profile of CCMS](https://www.bbb.org/us/tx/port-aransas/profile/commercial-property-management/ccms-resorts-port-aransas-0825-133498).

### Lede B — The Bhakta / 2019 / 7-0 lede

> On January 17, 2019, the Port Aransas City Council voted 7-0 to award the city's public-private hotel-conference center to Cinnamon Shore. The hospitality partner on the winning bid was ZJZ Hospitality. ZJZ's CEO, Deven Bhakta, was at that moment the Board Chairman of the Port Aransas Tourism Bureau — the 501(c)(6) that receives Hotel Occupancy Tax pass-through from the same city council that just voted his company the contract. The press archive contains no Form CIQ disclosing the overlap.

*Why this is strong:* unanimous vote, named partner, hard-documented dual role. *Why it's #2:* the structural overlap is harder to hold in one sentence than the Crawford lede. Sources: [South Jetty: Cinnamon Shore is city's pick](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/cinnamon-shore-is-citys-pick-for-hotel-conference-center-developer/); [South Jetty: Hentz reportedly resigns](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/hentz-reportedly-resigns-from-port-aransas-chamber/); [Bhakta Facebook board-chair message](https://www.facebook.com/pachamberofcommerce/photos/message-from-the-board-chairman:/2041206166091299/).

### Lede C — The 0.4 percent lede

> Of the 526 units inside Cinnamon Shore, the Nueces County Chief Tax Appraiser confirmed in 2021, exactly 2 qualified as homestead properties. That's 0.4 percent. In November 2021, the resort emailed those owners and asked them to "consider having one member of your family register to vote in Port A" — to defeat a beach-access road the city was planning to put on a public easement just south of the development. The Texas Secretary of State referred the matter to the Attorney General on March 17, 2022. The investigation status, as of April 2026, is unknown.

*Why this is strong:* the homestead ratio is the most cinematic single number in the fact base, and the voter-registration episode is the literal mechanism the working title invokes. *Why it's #3:* it's an episode lede, not a structural lede — it shows *what happens* before establishing *the architecture that allows it*. Source: [KRIS-TV Cinnamon Shore voter-registration coverage](https://www.kristv.com/news/6-investigates/port-aransas-beach-resort-fighting-beach-access-road-by-encouraging-residents-to-vote).

## Structural arc (the spine)

The piece runs **7 sections, lede through close.** Each section has one job. Aim ~250–400 words per section for ~2,000-word total — Heritage convention.

1. **Lede.** Crawford / CCMS-700 / 2023 rescission (per Lede A). One vote, one member, one missing filing.
2. **The structure.** How P&Z + Council + Tourism Bureau are *stocked*. Six of seven elected seats. P&Z appointed by the same council. Bureau board includes a sitting council member ex-officio + a Cinnamon Shore VP + a Palmilla rep + the Owens-family restaurant rep.
3. **The named overlaps.** Crawford-CCMS-STR; Bhakta-ZJZ-Tourism-Bureau-2019; Shimaitis-UWC-zoning; Christianson-Tradewinds-STR; Owens-six-businesses-tourism; Moore-American-Bank-lending. Flag what's documented vs. what's Winston-sourced (Underway, Lorette + spouse, "Lamkin handpicked Hentz").
4. **Tale of two outcomes.** Comparables. Tybee + Bar Harbor reformed (different mechanisms; same outcome). Sedona + Telluride + Aspen pre-reformed-late or never. The structural lesson: *geography isn't destiny; council composition is.*
5. **Cinnamon Shore case study.** The Two-Carve-Outs frame. 2003 Newport PUD failed → Cinnamon Shore took 60 acres in 2007 → McCombs took 150 acres in 2012 → 2019 vote pitted two carve-outs of the same failed plan. Plus the 0.4% homestead rate + the 2022 AG referral. Plus the migration of the conference-center site (awarded inside Cinnamon Shore in 2019, proposed at the Brookdale parcel two miles north by August 2020).
6. **The fix path.** Citizen-initiated ballot mechanism (Bar Harbor 1,260-840 + Provincetown petitioned-article model). Note the 2027 Charter Review Commission cycle as the next available reform window. Note the Tybee 4-1 ordinance + sunset-by-attrition mechanism as a council-driven path that *also* works when the council isn't industry-captured.
7. **The close.** Return to the thesis line. *"Leadership decides what happens to a town — not culture or destiny."* Refuse the framing that this is fate. Name the 2025/2027 charter cycle as the next decision point.

### § 1 Lede facts (Crawford / 2023 rescission)

- **Sept. 21, 2023:** council rescinded the 2021 ordinance requiring on-site residency for accessory-dwelling STR operation. Source: [South Jetty: City to consider residency for STRs](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/city-to-consider-residency-for-strs/).
- **Crawford = President, CCMS LLC; CCMS manages 700+ vacation rentals in Port Aransas.** Sources: [Port Aransas Chamber CCMS listing](https://www.portaransas.org/listing/ccms-llc/100/); [BBB CCMS profile](https://www.bbb.org/us/tx/port-aransas/profile/commercial-property-management/ccms-resorts-port-aransas-0825-133498); [South Jetty: CCMS 40th birthday](https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/ccms-observes-its-40th-birthday/).
- **Texas Local Gov Code Chapter 171 thresholds:** any one of (a) 10% ownership; (b) $15,000 fair market value; (c) 10% of gross income from the entity triggers "substantial interest" requiring a Form CIQ filing + abstention. Source: [Texas Local Gov Code § 171.002](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/LG/htm/LG.171.htm).
- **No indexed CIQ/CIS filing on the public record matched to this vote** — pending TPIA to City Secretary Francisca Nixon. Source: [City of Port Aransas Conflicts Disclosure page](https://cityofportaransas.org/government/city-secretary/conflicts-disclosure/).

### § 4 Tale-of-two-outcomes facts (comparables)

- **Tybee Island, GA (REFORMED):** June 13, 2024, 4-1 vote (Hosti dissent; Ploughe recused) prohibited new STR licenses in residential zones (R-1, R-1B, R-2). Sunset by attrition — no eviction. Mayor Brian West: *"This is an experiment of which we do not know the outcome.... let's let the experiment begin."* Pop. 3,400 (vs. PA 2,904); 1.575/2.994 = ~52% STR share. Sources: [WTOC: Tybee 4-1 vote](https://www.wtoc.com/2024/06/14/tybee-city-council-votes-remove-half-islands-short-term-rentals/); [WSAV: Tybee passes STR ordinance, West quote](https://www.wsav.com/news/local-news/city-of-tybee-passes-new-short-term-rental-ordinance/); [Connect Savannah: Q&A with Brian West](https://www.connectsavannah.com/community/qanda-with-brian-west-tybees-newly-inaugurated-mayor-21502246).
- **Bar Harbor, ME (REFORMED via ballot):** 2021 voters approved comprehensive STR ordinance restricting non-owner-occupied rentals to 9% of dwelling units. Vote 1,260 to 840. Maine Supreme Judicial Court upheld March 2024. *Citizen-initiated, not council-driven.* Sources: [Bar Harbor STR final report](https://www.barharbormaine.gov/DocumentCenter/View/4548/VR-final-report-12-30-2020-CORRECTED-1-5-2021); [Bangor Daily News on March 2024 high-court ruling](https://www.bangordailynews.com/2024/03/26/hancock/high-court-bar-harbor-short-term-rental-restrictions-n6hjn1me0n/).

Mayor Jablow: *"We're saying give us local control."* Sources: [12News Sedona affordability](https://www.12news.com/article/news/local/arizona/sedona-has-become-too-expensive-for-the-people-who-work-there-to-live-there/75-65b523bb-ebec-4928-9674-ebd641f552fb); [ABC15 Sedona housing emergency](https://www.abc15.com/news/state/sedona-leaders-say-vacation-rentals-are-driving-a-housing-shortage-emergency); [Sedona.biz aristocratic principality](https://sedona.biz/az-republic-calls-sedona-an-aristocratic-principality-without-affordable-housing/).
- **Telluride, CO (PRE-REFORM, late):** home-to-income ratio 37:1 in town; 16:1 county-wide. School-district issued $31.8M bond Nov. 2023 to construct workforce housing for its own teachers. School-district finance director: *"If there are no homes, I can't fill positions."* Sources: [Telluride Foundation: Regional Housing Issue](https://telluridefoundation.org/tellurides-regional-housing-issue/); [Fortune: Telluride housing crisis](https://fortune.com/2024/02/21/telluride-colorado-affordable-housing-municipal-debt-school-district/).
- **Aspen, CO (PRE-REFORM, terminal):** 42.9% housing vacancy (2020 census = second-home / investment); median single-family home $13.3M; 50-year drift; school enrollment down 6% over 5 years. Sources: [AspenTimes: housing spike enrollment](https://www.aspentimes.com/news/housing-spike-drives-declining-aspen-school-district-enrollment-official-says/); [Aspen Journalism: vacant homes](https://aspenjournalism.org/2020-census-data-highlights-the-relationship-between-resort-communities-and-downvalley-locales/).

## What the piece intentionally doesn't say (editorial restraint)

- **No imputation of intent.** No claim that any individual member voted with corrupt purpose. The piece reports the overlap; the reader draws the inference.
- **No claim of Chapter 171 violation absent CIQ archive verification.** Until the TPIA returns, the piece can describe the *conditions* that would constitute a § 171.004 special-economic-effect test without asserting one was triggered.
- **No use of off-record Winston-sourced claims as factual.** Underway P&Z, Lorette spouse, Lamkin-handpicked-Hentz — held back until records corroborate. Substitute the Bhakta concurrency overlap (which IS on-record) for the Lamkin-handpicked frame.
- **No interview with anyone named in the piece.** Per `feedback_pal_no_interviews.md` — actions are public-record, words off-record are not.
- **No native-vs-non-native framing.** The Mary Judson 2016 editorial line is the editorial north star — *interest*, not *birthright*. A salon owner born in Port A and a vacation-rental owner born in Atlanta are both stakeholders in the same regulated industry; the piece frames the question as one of *seat-by-business* not *seat-by-pedigree*.
- **No prediction.** The cautionary towns are pattern-matches, not deterministic forecasts. *"If Port A keeps these conditions, here is the trajectory the comparable towns followed"* — yes. *"This will happen to Port A"* — no.
- **No Charter Review Commission announcement-via-Dispatch.** The piece can name the 2027 cycle as the next reform window without claiming knowledge that any commission member intends to act.
