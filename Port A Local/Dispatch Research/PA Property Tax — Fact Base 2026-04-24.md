## Section 1 — Port Aransas ISD tax and recapture status

**District identity**
- PAISD serves Port Aransas, TX, in Nueces County. TEA category: "Non-metropolitan Fast Growing." 3 campuses: H.G. Olsen Elementary, Brundrett Middle, Port Aransas High School.
- Source: https://schools.texastribune.org/districts/port-aransas-isd/
- Superintendent: Sharon McKinney. Director of Business and Operations: Carol Sue Hipp. (Confirmed across multiple South Jetty articles below.)

**Chapter 49 / recapture status**
- **PAISD IS a Chapter 49 "excess local revenue" (recapture) district.** Source: https://tea.texas.gov/finance-and-grants/state-funding/excess-local-revenue
- **2023-24:** $28.8 million required recapture payment from PAISD property owners; TEA additionally invoiced $506,000 supplemental recapture tied to prior-year enrollment/attendance. Source: https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/paisd-sees-highest-enrollment-in-20-years/ (Dan Parker, Oct 9 2024)
- **2022-23:** ~$28 million estimate. Recapture rate from Superintendent McKinney: "80 cents per dollar" of M&O. Source: https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/mckinney-talks-taxes/ (Dan Parker, Oct 26 2022)
- **2021-22:** $20,091,230 per TXSC 2022 recapture-by-amount list. Source: https://www.txsc.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/2022-Total-Recapture-per-District-by-Amount.pdf
- **2019-20:** $16,280,098 per PAISD budget. Source: https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/salaries-up/ (Zach Perkins, Sep 4 2019)
- **2017 (post-Harvey):** 84% recapture rate. Source: https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/enrollment-bus-barn-more-on-trustee-agenda/
- **Trajectory:** Recapture grew from ~$16.3M (2019-20) to ~$28.8M (2023-24) — upward. Hipp reported "over $38 million" forwarded to the state across "the past two years" as of Feb 2023. Source: https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/texas-ranks-49th-in-per-student-spending/ (Carol Sue Hipp, Feb 8 2023)

**Direct on-the-record quote (McKinney, 2022):**
> "The majority of the taxes you pay to PAISD are not actually used for the education of children enrolled here in Port Aransas ISD."
> — Sharon McKinney, Superintendent, PAISD. Source: https://www.portasouthjetty.com/articles/mckinney-talks-taxes/

**Average-home PAISD tax breakdown (2022-23, per McKinney):**
- Total to PAISD: $2,431.63
  - M&O local retention: $443.33
  - Bond: $195.37
  - **State recapture: $1,793.93** (~74% of the PAISD bill)
  - Other local: $637.70

**Tax rates**
- **2019-20:** adopted total tax rate $1.0547 per $100 (down from $1.1180).
- **Uncertain — 2024-25 and 2025-26 M&O vs. I&S split NOT verified** from primary source. PAISD Required Postings page timed out. Re-verify at: https://paisd.gabbarthost.com/46283
- TEA cross-check sources: https://tea.texas.gov/finance-and-grants/state-funding/additional-finance-resources/school-district-adopted-tax-rates.pdf

**Budget**
- **2019-20:** $27,146,332 adopted.
- **Uncertain — 2024-25 and 2025-26 PAISD total budget NOT verified** from primary source.

**Enrollment**
- **Sept 16, 2024:** 582 students (Olsen 260, Brundrett 120, PAHS 202). Up from 530 in May 2024. Out-of-district transfers 151 (25.95%). Attendance rate 96.42%.
- **Texas Tribune Schools Explorer current figure:** 578 students. 74.2% white, 26.5% economically disadvantaged. Source: https://schools.texastribune.org/districts/port-aransas-isd/

**Tax-supported debt**
- FY 2025 outstanding: $33,190,000. Revenue-supported debt: $0. No new debt issued FY 2025. Source: https://data.brb.texas.gov/local/isd/2935.html

**Property wealth per student**

## Section 3 — Texas 89th Legislature (Regular Session, 2025) — property tax + school finance bills that moved

**Bills signed into law:**

- **SB 4 (Bettencourt, R-Houston)** — School district homestead exemption raised from $100,000 to $140,000. State covers lost district revenue. Contingent on Prop 13 (passed Nov 2025 with ~79% vote). Signed June 16, 2025. Retroactive to tax year 2025. Expires Jan 1, 2027 (sunset). Source: https://www.texaspolicyresearch.com/bills/89th-legislature-sb-4/
- **SB 23 (Bettencourt)** — Additional 65+/disabled school-tax homestead exemption from $10,000 to $60,000. Prop 11 (passed ~77%). Signed June 16, 2025.
- **HB 9 (Meyer)** — Business personal property exemption up to $125,000 per location. Contingent on HJR 1. Effective Jan 1, 2026. Biennial revenue loss estimate: >$566M.
- **HB 22** — Exempts intangible personal property from ad valorem. Effective Jan 1, 2026.
- **HB 2 (comprehensive public-education finance)** — $8.5B new school funding. Basic allotment raised from $6,160 to $6,380. Modifies §48.257 recapture formula on the margins for districts caught by collections-adjusted recapture. Signed June 20, 2025. Most provisions effective Sep 1, 2025.
- **HB 8** — Temporary $0.0331 per $100 school district tax rate compression. Effective 2025-26 tax year, expires Sep 1, 2027.
- **HB 30 (Troxclair)** — Replaces disaster-loophole voter-approval rate with narrowly defined "disaster debris rate." 3-year sunset.
- **HB 1533** — ARB process reforms (transparency, fairness standards, easier protest). Effective Sep 1, 2025.
- **SB 850** — Firm deadlines + automatic triggers for tax refund processing; interest penalties on delaying entities. Effective Sep 1, 2025.
- **SB 843 (Kolkhorst)** — TEA must create public database tracking school district bond elections, M&O rates, capital project expenditures.
- **SB 1502** — Prohibits school districts from adopting tax rates exceeding voter-rejected rates. Effective Jan 1, 2026.
- **SB 1453** — Allows taxing units to exceed debt rates with 60% voter approval after disclosure. Effective Jan 1, 2026.
- **HB 1392** — Extends tax payment due dates when collector offices are closed. Effective Jan 1, 2026.
- **HB 3093 / SB 1052 (Hinojosa)** — Gulf Coast county rate-calculation fix: 14 Gulf Coast counties (likely including Nueces — **bill text should be verified**) exclude contested property values from rate calculations under specified conditions. Hinojosa (D-McAllen, Sen. Dist. 20, covers Nueces) authored Senate companion. Effective immediately.

**Voter-ratified (November 2025)**
- Prop 13 (SB 4 — $140K homestead): ~79% approval. Source: https://ballotpedia.org/Texas_Proposition_13,_Increase_Homestead_Property_Tax_Exemption_Amendment_(2025)
- Prop 11 (SB 23 — 65+/disabled $60K): ~77% approval.
- Combined relief package cost to state: ~$10B. Average Texas homeowner school-tax savings estimate: ~$484/year.

**Chapter 49 / recapture reform**
- **No standalone Chapter 49 recapture-repeal bill passed the 89th regular session.** HB 2 modified §48.257 only on the margins.
- Major structural recapture reform was **proposed but NOT enacted.**

**Bills NOT enacted (backdrop)**
- Per Texas Policy Research summary: standalone bills targeting short-term rentals, appraisal caps, and Chapter 49 recapture reform did not pass. Source: https://www.texaspolicyresearch.com/89th-legislative-session-policy-brief-property-taxes/

**Port Aransas / Nueces representation in the 89th:**
- **Rep. Todd Hunter** (R-Corpus Christi, HD 32) — represents Port Aransas. Chairman, Calendars Committee. Member: Land and Resource Management, Public Education, Subcommittee on Academic and Career-Oriented Education. Source: https://www.capitol.state.tx.us/Members/MemberInfo.aspx?Leg=89&Chamber=H&Code=A3365
- **Uncertain — specific property-tax bills authored by Rep. Hunter not individually enumerated** in retrievable summaries. Check "Bills Authored" link on his member page.
- **Sen. Juan "Chuy" Hinojosa** (D-McAllen, SD 20 covers Nueces) — authored SB 1052 (rate-calc fix). Joint-authored property-tax-relief package bills.
