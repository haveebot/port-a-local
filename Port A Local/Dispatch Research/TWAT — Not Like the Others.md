## The angle

Most fishing tournaments are about the prize. TWAT is about the after — what gets handed to The Purple Door Sunday afternoon — and that completely reframes how the weekend feels. The piece sits a Port A reader in front of three tournaments side by side and asks them to feel the difference.

## The structure (rough)

1. **Open** — three tournaments on the Port A summer calendar:
   - **Deep Sea Roundup** — 90 years, Boatmen Inc., Piggy Perch, family-friendly, charitable but not charity-defining. The institution.
   - **Texas Legends Billfish** — Triple Crown leg, big yachts, big purses, big-game offshore. The high-stakes circuit stop.
   - **TWAT** — women-only, $130K+ to The Purple Door across 40+ years, no online merch store on purpose, family-run since 1984. The one where the cause IS the format.
2. **Pivot** — what makes TWAT structurally different. Not the women-only rule (that's the format), not the founder (Pete Fox started DSR-adjacent tournaments too), not the merch (downstream effect). The structural difference is *what the prize money funds afterward*.
3. **The Fox family** — Pete Fox 1984. Sandusky, Ohio → Corpus Christi 1958. Marine Surveyors. Fox Yacht Sales (203 W Cotter Ave, since 1989). Texas Saltwater Fishing Hall of Fame 2021. Chris Fox today. A single family's footprint across the tournament economy of an island.
4. **The Purple Door** — what $130K means in shelter terms. Cost-per-night, beds-per-year, services-funded. Source from purpledoortx.org's annual report or news coverage.
5. **The merch as evidence** — no online store. Day-1 tent. Shirts at HS football games in three states. The merch isn't a revenue play; it's the cultural footprint of a tournament that people willingly tell on themselves about.
6. **Land** — every tournament reflects what its sanctioning org cares about. Boatmen Inc. cares about scholarships and institutional continuity (DSR shows it). The pro circuit cares about purse and prestige (Texas Legends shows it). The Fox family cares about the shelter — and the entire tournament is shaped around making sure the check at the end is the loudest cheer of the night.
