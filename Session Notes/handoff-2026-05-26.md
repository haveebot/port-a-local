### Why it didn't convert — ranked

1. **Wrong intent window.** Spend was concentrated Wed 5/20 ($20.55) + Thu 5/21 ($20.09). Ads stopped Fri 5/22 evening — 14 hours BEFORE Memorial Day weekend started. The buyer of beach gear is "I'm packing tonight" or "I'm here on the beach right now" — not a Wednesday FB scroller 5 days out.
2. **Default audience meant heavy out-of-market reach.** No geo layer = Houston / Austin / DFW curiosity clicks from people who weren't coming this weekend.
3. **Creative had zero urgency.** "Memorial Day weekend on Port A — and your perfect beach day is one tap away." Brand awareness vibe. No "12 setups left for Saturday" / "book by Friday for guaranteed delivery" forcing function.
4. **/beach is a 568-line form for an $80 impulse buy.** Specific Setup Location, dates, full customer info — fine for high-intent buyers, a wall for FB-curiosity clicks.
5. **No retargeting.** ~99 people landed and bounced. Not one second-touch ad. Each impression was a cold first-touch with terminal conversion expectations.
6. **Single landing URL, both creatives** — can't tell whether placed-ad's $0.225 vs boost's $0.305 cost-per-click delta reveals anything actionable.

### What to test next weekend push

- Run **during** the weekend (Sat/Sun AM), not the workweek before
- Layer geo: Port A + Mustang Island + Corpus Christi 50mi radius
- Add urgency: "Today's setups deliver until 6pm" / "Saturday booked — 3 Sunday spots left"
- Add retargeting audience: /beach visitors from last 14d with a different second-touch creative
- Test a $25 single-chair single-day add-on as funnel entry — lower-friction landing, upsell to $80/$180/$450 on page
- A/B at minimum two creatives (Bron's social proof vs urgency/scarcity)

The honest read: not a creative failure or a price failure — a **timing + intent + audience match failure**. Ad fired into a feed of people who weren't in buying mode and stopped before buying-mode hours arrived.

## Cart-rental SMS routing bug — live discovery mid-session

### What happened

A live cart booking landed mid-session: **Ashlee S., 8-Passenger Golf Cart, Thursday June 11 → Sunday June 14 (3 days), DELIVERY**.

| UTC | Event |
|---|---|
| 19:53:52 | Booking confirmed. PAL fires PRIORITY first-look SMS to all 3 Bron's phones (primary + Bron Doyle's cell + Kristen) |
| 19:54:54 | **Bron Doyle's cell replies "Accept"** — 62 seconds after the SMS |
| 19:55:01 | Operator ping: `[Bron Doyle (cell) → PAL] Accept` — surfaced as a generic operator log, NOT processed as a claim |
| 19:55:46 | Separate beach booking lands (same customer, Ashlee S.) — fires CLAIM SMS to all 3 Bron's phones |
| 19:58:09 | Kristen replies "Claim beach set up!" — wins the beach lead |
| 20:00:43 | Bron Doyle's cell: *"I thought I claimed it before Kristen"* — he thought his earlier `Accept` was for the beach setup |
| 20:24:01–22 | First-look cron fires — open-blast SMS to **13 other cart vendors** |
| 20:24:13 | Operator ping: `[first-look ⏰] brons-beach-carts timed out on 8-Passenger Golf Cart — fan-out fired to 13 other-vendor SMS` |

### Root cause — two bugs

**Bug #1 (the smoking gun): inbound SMS webhook matcher order in `src/app/api/twilio/sms/inbound/route.ts`.** The matchers run insiders → **beach vendors** → cart vendors. All 3 Bron's phones are registered in BOTH `cart-vendors.ts` (single multi-phone vendor `brons-beach-carts`) AND `beach-vendors.ts` (3 separate beach rows `brons-reservations` / `brons-bron-cell` / `brons-kristen`). When the inbound webhook sees Bron's cell, `findBeachVendorByPhone` matches first, the beach handler's catch-all (lines 203–211) captures any non-CLAIM/non-STOP reply with a generic `[Vendor → PAL] ${body}` operator ping and `return twimlResponse()` — never reaches the cart-vendor branch. **Cart `ACCEPT` is completely unreachable for any Bron's phone.** Live since the beach-vendor roster split (~5/9).

**Bug #2 (collateral): `getFirstLookVendorsForSize(_cartSize)` in `src/data/cart-vendors.ts` line 467.** The parameter is `_cartSize` (prefixed underscore = unused). The filter ignores cart size entirely:

```ts
export function getFirstLookVendorsForSize(
  _cartSize: string,          // ← unused
): CartVendor[] {
  return cartVendors.filter(
    (v) =>
      v.active &&
      typeof v.firstLookMinutes === "number" &&
      v.firstLookMinutes > 0,
  );
}
```

Bron's `cartSizes: ["4", "6"]` — they don't do 8-passenger. But because the filter is a no-op, Ashlee's 8-pass lead still routed to them as first-look. They might have wanted it anyway (sub-rent / referral), but architecturally the priority shouldn't have routed there.

### Recovery action taken

Sent 16 SMS total via Twilio direct (workspace-mediated, since the in-app claim flow was the broken path):

| Group | Count | Message |
|---|---:|---|
| 13 fanned-out cart vendors | 13 | "Port A Local: The 8-pass cart lead for Jun 11-14 was just claimed. Thanks for the quick eye — next one's still up for grabs. STOP to opt out." |
| Bron's team (3 phones) | 3 | Full lead confirm — Ashlee S. · 8-Pass Cart · Jun 11–14 · DELIVERY · $20 off · logistics 24–48hr before · plus the clarification that the beach setup was Kristen's separate claim |

Pre-send verified inbound to PAL — no other vendor had replied ACCEPT in the open-blast window before our intercept. All 16 dispatches `accepted` by Twilio. Lead is operationally locked. DB-side `cart_rental_first_look_pending` row remains in `status='expired'` — there's no application-layer lock on the open-blast phase; operator-side SMS awareness handles it. If any of the 13 still replies ACCEPT in the next hours, it'll just operator-ping you and you'll know to ignore.
